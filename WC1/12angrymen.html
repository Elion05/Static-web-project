Summary

Twelve jurors are tasked with deciding the fate of a young man accused of murder. Initially, all but one are convinced of his guilt. However, as Juror #8 meticulously unravels the evidence, the jurors confront their own biases, assumptions, and personal beliefs. This gripping courtroom drama explores themes of justice, prejudice, and the power of reasonable doubt.

Featured Review

12 Angry Men is a timeless masterpiece exploring justice, prejudice, and moral responsibility.

Sidney Lumet’s sharp direction and a stellar cast create a tense, thought-provoking drama. Henry Fonda shines as Juror #8, standing for empathy and logic. Set entirely in a jury room, the film is a compelling study of human nature and the courage to challenge bias—a cinematic gem as relevant today as in 1957
